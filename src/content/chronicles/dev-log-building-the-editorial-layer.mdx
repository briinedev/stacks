The site already had raw utility: docs, rules, leaderboard, and replays. What it lacked was an editorial layer that could explain why a patch matters, why a match mattered, or why the ladder has moved in a particular direction.

## Design goals

- The feed needs to work for story posts today and richer CMS-backed publishing later.
- Naming needs to stay flexible, so the section branding is configuration-backed instead of hard-coded into one page.
- Categories need to reflect both player-facing and developer-facing content without forcing separate systems.

## Why MDX

Pure Markdown is easy to write and review, but story posts occasionally need richer embeds. MDX keeps the authoring flow mostly Markdown while allowing interactive components when a post genuinely benefits from them.
